PROPHET MUHAMMED

The life and legacy of prophet Muhammed

Prophet Muhammed (peace be upon him) was born in Mecca, Arabia in the year570 CE. He was a member of the Quraysh tribe, which was very influential tribe in Mecca at that time.Muhammed's father, Abdullah,was a merchant who died before Prophet Muhammed was born, and his mother Aminah, was a member of Banu Hashim clan and she died when Prophet Muhammed was six years old

Early Life

Muhammed was raised by his grandfather,Abu-Muttalib, who was a respected leader in Mecca. After his grandfather's death, Muhammed was taken by his uncle, Abu Talib, who becamehis guardian. Muhammed grew up in a family of modest means, but he was known for his honesty, kindness, an intergrity. As a young man Muhammed worked as a merchant,travelling to various parts of Arabia and syria. He was known for his fairness and trustworthiness, and he quickly gained a reputation as a reliable and honest bussinessman.Muhammed married a wealthy widow named Khadijah when he was 25 years old, who was 15 years his senior.The marriage was a happy one, and Khadijah become a source of support and comfort for muhammed throughout his life.

The call to prophethood

In 610 CE,when Muhammad was 40 years old, he recieved his first revelation from Allah through Angel Jubril.This event marked the beginning of Muhammed's prophetic career,and it is significant events in islamic history.
The revelation came to Muhammed in a cave on Mount Hira, where he has gone to meditate and pray. Angel Jubril appeared to Muhammed and commanded him to "read" the words of Allah. Muhammed, who was illiterate, replied that he could not read, but Angel Jubril repeated the command, and told him to read, and Muhammed began to recite the words of the Quran
Over the next twenty three years, Muhammed recieved a series of revelations from Allah, which were later compiled into the Quran. These revelations formed the basis of Islam, and they provided guidance on a wide range of topics, including worship, morality,and governance.

The early years of Islam

After recieving his first revelation, Muhammed began to preach the message of Islam to his family and friends .He faced significant opposition and persecution, particularly from the leaders of Quraysh tribe, who saw Islam as a threat to their power and influence.
Despite the challenges Muhammed continue to preach and teach, and he attracted a small but dedicated group of followers.These early Muslim, including Muhammed's wife Khadijah, his cousin Ali, and his friend Abu Bakr, became the core of the Islamic community.
In 622 CE, Muhammed and his followers migrated from Mecca to Medinah, a city located about 200 miles to the north.This event known as the Hijra, marked the beginning of Islamic calender and the establishment of Islamic community.

The establishment of the Islamic community

In Medinah, Muhammed established the first Islamic community, which was based on the principle of justice, inequality, and compassion He created a system of government which included a council of advisors and a judiciary, and he established a set of regulations that governed the behaviour of muslims
Muhammed also established the institution of prayer, which become a central part of islamic worship. He designated the direction of the Kaaba in Mecca as the Qibla(the direction of prayer) and he established the five daily prayers, which are still observed by muslims today

The conquests and expansion of Islam

In the years following the Hijra, Muhammed and his followersfaced significant challenges, including battling with the Quraysh tribe and other enemies of Islam. Despite these challenges, the islamic community continued to grow, the islamic challenges continue to grow and expand, Muhammed became a skilled millitary leader and strategist.
In 630CE, Muhammed and his followers conquered Mecca, and the city became the centre of Islamic learning and culture. Over the next few years, Muhammed and his followers expanded their control over much of Arabia, and Islam became a major force in the region

The farewell pilgrimage and the death of Muhammed

In 632 CE,Muhammed made his final pilgrimage to Mecca 🕋, which became known as the Farewell pilgrimage. During this pilgrimage, Muhammed delivered a sermon in which he summarized the key principles of Islam and emphasized the importance of justice, compassion, and equality.
After the pilgrimage, Muhammed returned to Medinah, where he became ill and died on a Monday, and the most well-known view is that it occured on 12th Rabee'al-Awwal 11 AH. This corresponds to the year 632 CE.He was 63 years old when he passed away.His death marked the end of an era, and it had a profound impact on the Islamic community.

Conclusion
